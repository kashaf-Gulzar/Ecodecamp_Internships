Masoor Pulao

Key Ingredients

    For the Masoor Curry

  • 1/2 cup whole massor red lentils
  • 1 green chilli
  • 2 teasppon ginger & garlic paste
  • 2 medium onions finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes finely chopped
  • 2-3 teaspoons red chilli powder adjust to taste
  • 2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Oil(or ghee for a non vegan version)
  • Salt to taste

    • For the Rice

  • 1-1/2 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 stick cassia bark or cinnamon
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 cardamons slightly bruised

    • To Garnish

  • 1 medium-small onions, finely sliced
  • 1/4 cup cashew nut halves
  • 1/8th cup raisins
  • Chopped fresh coriander
  • Oil (or ghee for a non vegan version) for frying

Cooking Steps

  • Step 1: Wash and soak the masoor in plenty of water for at least 8 hours or overnight, then discard the water and refresh with fresh water.
  • Step 2: In a pressure cooker, heat the oil or ghee and fry the slit green chillies. Cover the pan to prevent oil from spluttering. Add the ginger-garlic paste as well and fry it on a low heat for half a minute or until the raw smell vanishes.
  • Step 3: Toss in the sliced onions and let them fry till translucent. Then add the tomatoes and salt and fry till they turn mushy and oil begins to seprate.
  • Step 4: Add the turmeric coriander, cumin and red chilli powders and fry for half a minute. Drain the whole masoor completely and add it in. Give everything a good mix and add a cup of water. Check salt and bring the mixture to a boil.
  • Step 5: Cover pressure cook on full heat for two whistles. Remove the cooker from the heat and leave it undistributed until you are able to remove the whistle easily. Open, stir and keep aside( if it is not yet tender, put it back on the heat and give it another whistle). The masoor dal should be tender but not overcooked or mushy.
  • Step 6: To make the rice, first wash the rice in 2-3 changes of water, drain and keep aside. Bring 3 cups of water to a boil. You may use plain water or chicken or vegetable stock ( or bouillon (stock) cube dissolved in water)
  • Step 7: Heat the ghee or oil in a heavy based kadai.wok and add the whole spices - cloves cinnamon or cassia bark, bayleaf and cardamons Let them sizzle on a low heat for a few seconds
  • Step 8: Add the washed & drained rice and fry it for a couple of minutes. When the ladle begins to feel a little heavy add the freshly boiled water/stock mixture and turn up the heat. Give the micture a good boil-let it bubble for a minutes or so. Then reduce the heat completely to a sim and cover with a well fitting lid so on stream can escape. Keep a timer for exactly 5 minutes. Then turn off the heat nad let the rice cook undistributed for 15 minutes. After the time is up, open the pan and gently fluff up the rice using a fork. Cover and keep aside
  • Step 9: While the rice is cooking you can heat oil or ghee in a small pan and fry the items for garnishing. First fry the sliced onions till they turn golden brown and remove on a plate lined with kitchen tissur. Next fry the raisins & nuts separately until they turn golden. Remove on the plate.
  • Step 10: Once the rice is ready, layer 1/3rd of the portion in a serving dish. Top it with a layer of cooked masoor dal mixture.Add another layer rice and then a layer of the masoor dal. Continue till all the rice and masoor dal have been used up. Garnish with the fried onions, raisins & nuts. Sprinke with chopped coriander and serve hot with raitha or salan (or curry)
